# AI Tips Social Media — Content Style Guide

## Voice & Tone
- **Casual, direct, founder-voiced.** Short sentences. No fluff.
- Write like a smart friend explaining why this matters over coffee.
- Slightly urgent but never fear-mongering.
- Confident but not arrogant. You're sharing, not lecturing.

## Audience
- Adults 25–45 who are AI-curious but not technical
- Parents who want to understand what their kids will grow up with
- Professionals wondering how AI affects their career
- People who've heard of ChatGPT but haven't gone deeper

## Hook Formulas That Work
1. **Number hooks:** "5 AI tools that replaced my $200/month subscriptions"
2. **Curiosity gaps:** "Most people don't know ChatGPT can do this"
3. **Mild urgency:** "If you haven't tried this yet, you're already behind"
4. **Before/after:** "I used to spend 3 hours on reports. Now it takes 12 minutes."
5. **Insider knowledge:** "OpenAI quietly released something huge last week"
6. **Parent angle:** "Your kid's homework just changed forever"

## Hooks to AVOID
- Questions as openers ("Did you know...?" "Have you ever...?")
- Vague inspiration ("AI is changing everything")
- Press release tone ("We're excited to announce...")
- Clickbait without payoff

## Banned Words/Phrases
- "Dive into"
- "Game-changer"
- "Revolutionize"
- "Harness the power of"
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "Unlock the potential"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)

## Instagram Specifics
- Caption length: 150–300 words (enough for value, short enough to read)
- Always end with a CTA: "Save this for later", "Tag someone who needs this", "Drop a 🔥 if you're trying this tonight"
- Hashtags: 3–5 max, placed at the end
- Good hashtags: #AITips #ChatGPT #AIForBeginners #TechTips #FutureOfWork
- Bad hashtags: #AI #Tech #Innovation (too generic)

## Twitter/X Specifics
- Max 280 characters
- Front-load the hook — first 100 characters must stop the scroll
- 2–3 hashtags max, weave into the text or place at end
- Threads: Hook tweet → 3–5 value tweets → CTA tweet

## Carousel Guidelines (Instagram)
- Slide 1: HOOK ONLY. Bold text, dark background. Must stop the scroll.
- Slides 2–6: One point per slide. Short text. Visual hierarchy.
- Final slide: CTA + account handle
- Design: Dark backgrounds, bold sans-serif typography, accent color highlights

## Image Concept Descriptions
When describing images for manual creation, specify:
- Background color/style (prefer dark, clean)
- Text placement and hierarchy
- Any icons or simple graphics
- Mood: authoritative but approachable
